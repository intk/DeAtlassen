Zuyder Kerck

The Zuiderkerk church, completed in 1614 and located between Antoniesbreestraat and Zandstraat, was the first of three Protestant churches which the master builder Hendrick de Keyser designed for the city of Amsterdam. Not only that, it was also the first church built in Amsterdam for the Calvinists 'after the Roman [Catholic] doctrine was subdued,' wrote Olfert Dapper approvingly. The first stone was laid in 1603 by Mayor Bikker's son. De Keyser himself was buried here in 1621, and his gravestone can still be seen.
