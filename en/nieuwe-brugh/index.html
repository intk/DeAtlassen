Nieuwe Brugh

According to Dapper, the Nieuwebrug ('New Bridge') owes its name to the fact that in the seventeenth century, it was the most recently built of the three bridges over the Damrak canal. Dapper also writes that the bridge had 'stone supports on both sides at the front end'. This was unusual because most bridges at that time were made entirely of wood, leaving them vulnerable to fire and rot. The latest incarnation of the Nieuw Brug crosses in front of Stationsplein square and supports pedestrians, cyclists, and vehicles.
