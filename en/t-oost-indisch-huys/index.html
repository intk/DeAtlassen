t Oost Indisch Huys

In 1603 the Dutch East India Company established its headquarters in the Bushuis, on the corner of Kloveniersburgwal and Oude Hoogstraat. The attics were used to store spices from Asia. In the abattoir next door, 'oxen were slaughtered, packed in barrels and sent to the outlying districts.' The Dutch East India Company grew and expanded and by 1633, the complex consisted of four wings surrounding a courtyard. The building, known as the Oost-Indisch Huis (East India House), served as a warehouse, offices, and auction house. The governing board, the Heeren XVII (Lords Seventeen), held their meetings there, and sailors could sign up to join ships' crews in the inner courtyard.
