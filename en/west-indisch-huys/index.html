West Indisch Huys

In 1647 the Dutch West India Company (WIC) had to move out of its original headquarters on Herenmarkt square due to a lack of funds. The West India Company moved to this building near the island of Rapenburg, where the Oudeschans canal flows into the River IJ. The WIC had its headquarters here between 1647 and 1674. Today it is known as the West-Indisch Pakhuis (West India Warehouse). The building dates from 1642 and was originally used to store WIC trade goods. The ground floor was used for storing and pressing hides from trading posts like New Amsterdam (today's New York City).
