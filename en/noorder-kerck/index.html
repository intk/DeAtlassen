Noorder Kerck

The Noorderkerk church was designed in the Amsterdam Renaissance style by Hendrick de Keyser. According to Olfert Dapper, the first stone was laid in 1620 by the son of the city's master mason, and the church was 'entirely complete in three years' time, which is miraculous...'. In the early seventeenth century, the population of the western canal ring and Jordaan districts grew so rapidly that the city council commissioned the construction of the Noorderkerk and the Westerkerk churches at the same time, to serve as places of worship for those districts.
